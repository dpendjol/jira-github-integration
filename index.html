Mantis Shrimp

The mantis shrimp is a marine crustacean that belongs to the order Stomatopoda. They are known for their incredible eyesight, powerful claws, and lightning-fast strikes.

Appearance

Mantis shrimp have a uniquely shaped body and are one of the most colorful animals in the ocean. They come in a range of colors including bright greens, blues, oranges, and pinks.

Behavior

Mantis shrimp are aggressive predators that use their powerful claws to hunt prey. They are known to have one of the fastest movements in the animal kingdom and can strike their prey with incredible force.

Habitat

Mantis shrimp can be found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world. They are typically found in shallow, sandy areas or in coral reefs.

Interesting Facts

  • Mantis shrimp have 16 color receptors in their eyes, compared to humans who only have 3.
  • They are also known as "thumb splitters" because of their powerful claws.
  • Mantis shrimp can punch with the force of a .22 caliber bullet.
